The product

A cockpit is not a dashboard.

A dashboard shows you what happened. A cockpit tells you what to do about it — and automates the part you'd rather not do by hand.

What you have today
Excel files, exported reports, and someone spending three days a month assembling numbers that are already out of date when they land.
What a cockpit does

Before / after

Two cockpits, two industries, one pattern.

Every cockpit starts as a manual process someone was dreading. Here's what changed.

Hospitality · premium resorts
Revenue Cockpit
■ Before

Booking data lived in the PMS but had to be pulled and consolidated by hand in Excel every week. No consolidated view across the group's properties. Leadership had no fast answer to "how much did we sell yesterday, and on which channels?" Reporting to head office took hours.

● After

One revenue cockpit wired to the PMS. Daily pickup, 12-month forecast, occupancy, ADR, RevPAR, channel split, cancellation tracking, automatic alerts. Leadership opens their phone and reads the business in 30 seconds. The weekly manual report is gone.

Construction · materials
Site Operations Cockpit
■ Before

The whole chain — quote to site delivery — ran on carbon PDF forms, a shared spreadsheet and manual re-entry. No real-time view of what was dispatched to which site. Delivered-vs-planned control was done by hand, with undetected variances slipping through.

● After

One app drives the entire chain. The project manager creates a dispatch from mobile, the storekeeper is notified, stock decrements live, and a dashboard flags any variance beyond threshold instantly. Material quantities are auto-calculated from surface areas. Real-time view on every active site.
